Quick Summary
1
Community

CHP Outreach & Continuous Enrollment

Community Health Promoters (CHPs) reach people where they are — homes, churches, markets, workplaces, and boda groups — every month, not just during campaigns.

  • Mobile web prescreening via QR code or link → results sent to Supabase
  • Cases automatically assigned to a CHP by Community Health Unit (CHU)
2
Digital Platform

Decision Support Console

A bounded AI tool helps CHPs manage their caseload without overstepping clinical boundaries.

  • Priority routing: Green / Amber / Red triage flags
  • Counselling scripts for common barriers: fear, cost, distance
  • Appointment booking, automated reminders, and follow-up task lists
3
HealthPod — Monthly "Cancer Week"

Nurse-Led Community Screening

A structured two-day-per-group service window, close to where people live.

Women (2 days) VIA/VILI · Clinical Breast Exam (CBE) · Rapid FIT
Men (2 days) CBE · Rapid FIT · Targeted PSA (lab-supported)
  • Structured checklists + optional voice note documentation
  • Clinician escalation protocol for any abnormal findings
4
Clinician Review

Referral & Navigation

Abnormal findings trigger a closed-loop referral process so no patient slips through the gap.

  • Auto-generated referral pack sent with the patient
  • CHP remains the named navigator and follows up until attendance is confirmed
5
Nazareth Hospital

Definitive Diagnosis & Treatment

Nazareth Hospital provides all confirmatory diagnostics and clinical treatment. Results feed back into the system to close the loop for the referring CHP.

6
Ongoing

Annual Recall & Quality Improvement

  • Annual FIT due-date reminders for eligible clients
  • Follow-up completion tracked per CHP and CHU
  • Data feeds a replication toolkit for scaling to new CHUs
🏥
What is a HealthPod?

A HealthPod is a nurse-led screening delivery point and workflow model that brings specified services closer to communities — implemented as a physical site when needed, and always implemented as a structured service cadence ("Cancer Week") with standardised staffing, documentation, escalation, and follow-up.

What the stepped-wedge shows

In the control arm, Nazareth Hospital is both the screening site and treatment centre. In the intervention arm, community-proximate HealthPod screening is inserted before Nazareth referral — reducing the burden on the hospital while bringing care closer to patients. Outcomes across both arms are compared to evaluate the model.

  • 🏘️
    CHPs enroll continuously within CHUs using a mobile web tool. Cases auto-assign by CHU so every person has a named community health promoter responsible for them.
  • 💻
    A decision-support console helps CHPs prioritise cases, book appointments, use counselling scripts, and track follow-up tasks — within clearly bounded, non-clinical limits.
  • 🩺
    Monthly HealthPod "Cancer Week" sessions deliver nurse-led VIA/VILI, clinical breast exams, and lab-supported FIT/PSA testing — close to where people live. Clinicians review all abnormal results.
  • 🏥
    Nazareth Hospital provides definitive care. Referrals are closed-loop: CHPs track attendance, and outcomes feed back into the platform. Eligible clients receive annual FIT recall reminders.
